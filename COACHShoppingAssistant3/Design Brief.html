Shopping Assistant — Design Direction

Mobile web first · Desktop · 3 directions to compare

The job

A warm personal stylist, living in a floating bubble.

A conversational assistant on coach.ae that helps shoppers discover products ("a bag in my favourite colour"), find the perfect gift, sort out payment & BNPL questions (Tabby / Tamara), and answer FAQs (delivery, returns, stores). It opens with a proactive greeting after a few seconds, takes both quick-tap chips and free text, shows products in a swipeable carousel, and lets you add to bag right inside the thread or jump to the full product page.

This is an original assistant designed to live inside Coach's visual language — not a copy of any existing Coach UI.

Three directions to compare

Same system, three temperaments.

All three are fully clickable mobile prototypes (with a desktop view too). You'll be able to mix & match — the goal is to react to atomic choices, not pick one winner.

01

Classic

By the book.

White panel, black header with the COACH wordmark, square chips & cards. Reads as a native, trustworthy extension of the site. The safe baseline.

02

Editorial

A stylist's note.

Warm ivory, large serif welcome ("Hello. Let's find something you'll love."), generous whitespace, image-forward. Feels personal and luxe.

03

Boutique

The "C" monogram, alive.

Most expressive — the gold "C" as avatar, layered elevated panel, lookbook-style full-bleed carousel, tactile micro-interactions.

The four conversations

What each flow does

  • Discovery "A bag in my favourite colour" → asks colour / style / budget via chips + text → swipe carousel of matches → add to bag inline or view details.
  • Gifting Natural conversation — recipient, occasion, budget surface organically, no visible wizard steps → curated picks → gift-note nudge.
  • Payments Tabby / Tamara explainers, payment methods, "is it interest-free?" → clear answers with the BNPL split, escalation to a human if needed.
  • FAQs Delivery, returns, store locator → concise answers with a link out to the full policy page.

If this direction feels right, I'll build the mobile prototype first and show it to you early — then layer in the three temperaments and a Tweaks panel so you can dial tone, density and accents live.
